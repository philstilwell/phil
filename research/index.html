Research program

Calibrated confidence and repairable rationality.

A connected program spanning epistemology, credencing, metaethics, evidence standards, rational disagreement, and AI-assisted inquiry.

Method: expose evidence, defeaters, alternatives, confidence levels, and update conditions.

Aim: make public reasoning more auditable without flattening uncertainty.

Conceptual framework

Four connected lines of inquiry

The topics share one diagnostic question: does confidence remain answerable to available evidence?

Epistemology

Credence before binary knowledge.

Belief is treated as a graded evidential posture, revisable in response to uncertainty, alternatives, and defeaters.

Credencing

Make confidence auditable.

Claims should state their support, confidence range, live alternatives, and the evidence that would trigger an update.

Metaethics

Value without a moral realm.

Moral language compresses affect, preference, vulnerability, social coordination, and proposed regulation without requiring stance-independent moral facts.

Rationality / AI

Prosthetic, not oracle.

AI can widen search and simulate objections, but its output still requires source traceability, calibration, and human update discipline.

Epistemic architecture

Evidence, perception, credence, and speech are separate layers.

Separating the layers avoids treating disagreement, uncertainty, ignorance, rationalization, and bad faith as one blunt failure.

Evidence available

What information, base rates, testimony, observations, and alternatives are actually accessible?

Evidence perceived

Which parts of that situation does the agent notice, understand, or suppress?

Confidence assigned

Does the resulting credence track the evidence as the agent understands it?

Commitment expressed

Does public language preserve uncertainty, or inflate partial belief into flat certainty?
